Overview

TTraceable explores the current issue of online safety. The majority of people interact with and use technology without knowing the factors of being vulnerable. We are given options to protect our online safety, but those options are not always guaranteed. We are easily being manipulated by trackers, phishing messages, spam attacks, duplication of social media accounts, pop-up adds and many other cyber-attacks. Many tools of manipulation create the feeling of paranoia amongst individuals, who sense that their online presence was intruded. People tend to cover up their web cams, use pop-up add blockers, and use private browsing tools.

Traceable bring awareness to the idea that someone is watching us and tracing our online prints. Web cookies are a part of methods used to trace individual identities and online history. The goal of this project is to explore the idea of identities being traced and simulate the process of identity vulnerability, while using the internet. Mediums used will include both digital and print. Digital media will include a website that houses the simulation and informative visual explorations of the tracing process. The simulation will be created to involve the audience’s interaction and participate in the process of events that lures in personal online activity.

MThe simulation will contain several of questions and examples that tries to get the user to input information and responds with a generated response. Four posters will be displayed in order to demonstrate visual explanations of the traceable identity and presence of disruption that is centered around one’s experience while being vulnerable online. The printed posters will also support ideas expressed in digital media. The targeted audience is the general public, which connects to anyone who uses technology and the internet on any device.
