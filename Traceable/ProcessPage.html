Research Process & Definitions


PHISHING

The act of fraud is committed by sending emails that are false. Phishing scams use reproductions of actual companies that exist. Scammer’s try to make user’s vulnerable by causing them to reveal personal information such as credit cards and passwords. Phishing scammers have to ability to steal money. Beware that phishing scams can contain ransomware, and cause you to loose control and access to your own device. To learn more about phishing and safety you can visit the Federal Trade Commission website at: https://www.consumer.ftc.gov/articles/0003-phishing.



COOKIES

Data collected by the Internet's browser. The browser has the ability to track and access the Internet's server. Cookies can also be used to track users and access their servers. The data consist of small files stored onto the user’s computer. Cookies are used and collected by websites that the user visits. Sometimes cookies are helpful, but they also can be used against you. To learn more about the function and purpose of web cookies visit:https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/HTTP/Cookies.



POP-UP'S

They are Online advertisements that are usually unwanted. Ads have the ability to appear in a fast motion in order to catch the user’s attention. They are usually seen as a distraction. Pop-up blockers can be used in order to avoid these advertisements. Pop-up's can range from any type of ad. They also usaully appear in a suddenly fast motion. You should be cauttous of pop ad's and opt out when deciding to click on them. There are five common types of pop ads which are: Text Ads, Banners, Floating Ads, Timed, and Scroll Ads.



CANVAS FINGERPRINTING

A form of tracking that is used by many Online Websites. It’s intended purpose is to enhance web graphics using javascript. It also eliminates the use of web cookies. Canvas fingerprinting is used by the HTML 5 API, and it remembers the user’s web activity. They have the ability to indentify individual user's. The data of the user is collected and used for a range of purposes. Canvas Fingerprinting is also sometimes called browser fingerprinting.



PRIVATE BROWSING

A function that is used to browse Online without being traced. Private browsing will not store passwords, cookie data, and history. But, some websites still possess the ability to install hidden trackers on to your browser. Some private browsers can also block Ads that contain trackers. The user's browsing data will not be stored and collected.



SITE DATA

A site data collects and stores files, images, and cookies from websites the user visits in their browser. Site data can consume small amounts of storage but, are not significant amounts. The user has the option to delete site data, but the process will be repeated every time a website is visited. Every time the user visits a site, data will be downloaded, because site data is needed to display of the site and usability.



SPAM

The process of sending unsolicited messages and emails to people. Similar to phishing, spam can steal personal information. It is common to receive spam messages that can be sent to anyone without gaining their permission.



HACKING

The act of stealing information and data from a computer. Acts involving hacking are committed by skilled programmers. The possess to skill to invade most computer system and gain all types of data. 



PRIVATE INTERNET ACCESS

A private browsing network that uses IP connectivity. It allows the user to secure browsing activity. The vpn server has to be connected to a network. VPN’s has flexibility by allowing the user to stay anonymous and safe on public wifi. IP addresses can be hidden and encrypt browsing traffic.



Ad BLOCKERS

Are tools used to protect your data. It can be used to remove Ads in browsers and on websites. Ads have the ability to bombard your computer with sounds, pictures, web graphics and banners. Ad blockers are helpful to stop ads from tracking your activity.



RANSOMWARE

Malicious software that is used to block access to computer systems. In order to gain access back it requires payment. Individual users and large corporations can be targeted by ransomware.



FILE VAULT

Used as a disk encryption system that prevents access to the computers start up disk. The entire drive can become encrypted when the entire file vault is in use. Data and information stored onto the disk can be recovered through password access.



TRACKERS

Collecting data and fingerprints from browsers traffic online. IP addresses can be tracked, and including websites. Trackers have the abaility to colletc data that is not encrypted. Trackers can be produced through any website or organization.



DIGITAL PRIVACY

Having or gaining privacy for yourself by being online. Digital privacy is centered around your personal identity. Privacy online is a current issue because many personal indentities of online users have been comprised. Our digital privacy is sold and collected by large corporations.
